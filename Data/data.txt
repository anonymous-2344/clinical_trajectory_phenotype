    <h1>Data Description</h1>

    <h2>Data Source</h2>
    <p>
        Our primary data source is the <strong>MIMIC-III v1.4</strong> database, which contains detailed information on over 
        <strong>40,000 patients</strong> admitted to critical care units at <strong>Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center (BIDMC)</strong> 
        between 2001 and 2012. The database is publicly available under pre-existing 
        <strong>Institutional Review Board (IRB)</strong> approval. Researchers can access it by completing the 
        <em>“Data or Specimens Only Research”</em> training course offered by the 
        <strong>Collaborative Institutional Training Initiative (CITI)</strong>.
    </p>
    <p>
        For more details, visit: 
        https://physionet.org/content/mimiciii/1.4/.
    </p>

    <h2>Cohort Selection</h2>
    <p>
        Our experiments were conducted on a cohort of <strong>1,660 sepsis-related hospital admissions</strong>.
        To comply with data regulations, the file <code>Admissions.xlsx</code> includes only the list of 
        <code>HADM_IDs</code> selected for our experiments. Using these IDs, you can extract:
    </p>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Demographics</strong>: from <code>ADMISSIONS.csv</code> and <code>PATIENTS.csv</code></li>
        <li><strong>Nursing Notes</strong>: from <code>NOTEEVENTS.csv</code></li>
        <li><strong>Vitals & Lab Events</strong>: from <code>CHARTEVENTS.csv</code> and <code>LABEVENTS.csv</code></li>
    </ul>

    <h2>Structured Data</h2>
    <p>
        The folder <code>structured_data</code> contains <strong>27 structured parameters</strong>, representing day-wise worst values after our preprocessing pipeline.
    </p>

    <h2>Question Bank</h2>
    <p>
        The file <code>Question_bank.xlsx</code> includes a list of <strong>discriminative contrastive questions</strong> generated by our model.
    </p>
